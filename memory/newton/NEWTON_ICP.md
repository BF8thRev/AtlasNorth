# Newton ICP — Ideal Customer Profile

**Source:** Copy of ICP | Newton (G-Drive: Newton Background Folder)
**Updated:** 2026-03-06
**Category:** [Newton] — Sales Strategy

---

## ICP Summary

Newton sells to cannabis extraction teams under margin pressure that:
- Already have SOPs and data
- **Lack real-time visibility during execution**
- Rely on post-run analysis, spreadsheets, operator narratives
- Feel blind during batches and frustrated by repeated debates after execution

**Buying trigger:** Uncertainty becomes more expensive than change.

---

## Active ICP: Mid to Large Multi-Operator Extraction Organizations Under Margin Pressure

### Firmographics

- **Industry:** Cannabis extraction and processing
- **Size:** 2-10 extraction systems OR multi-facility operators
- **Revenue:** Meaningful but declining/unstable margins
- **Structure:** Often vertically integrated or aspiring to be
- **Geography:** Regulated state markets with compliance pressure

### Buyer Titles (Decision Committee)

- VP Operations (economic buyer)
- Director of Extraction (day-to-day user)
- COO (economic buyer)
- Head of Manufacturing (day-to-day user)
- CEO (economic buyer)

---

## Situational Triggers (When They Engage)

Prospects engage when at least ONE of these is present:

1. Yields vary despite identical inputs
2. Operators blame material, SOPs, or equipment interchangeably
3. Post-run analysis happens DAYS or WEEKS later
4. Teams rely on spreadsheets or tribal knowledge
5. Leadership feels blind during the run
6. Extraction throughput looks fine but profitability is eroding
7. Prior bad experience with consultants or AI tools

---

## Core Problems Newton Solves

1. **Lack of real-time visibility during the batch**
2. **No clear attribution for yield changes**
3. **Slow feedback loops causing repeated mistakes**
4. **Inability to separate operator behavior from material/SOP issues**
5. **High cognitive load on senior operators and directors**
6. **Decisions made after damage is already done**

---

## What They SAY Out Loud (vs. What's Actually Happening)

| What They Say | What's Actually Happening |
|---|---|
| "We already track our runs" | Data exists but is fragmented and unused |
| "We know our process pretty well" | Variability is normalized instead of explained |
| "Yields move because cannabis is variable" | Teams are reacting instead of correcting |
| "Operators would never use another tool" | Senior staff act as human feedback loops |
| "We don't want another dashboard" | Problems discovered only after revenue is lost |
| "Software hasn't helped us before" | Confidence in decision-making is lower than stated |

---

## Primary Objections

1. Skepticism of software-driven insights
2. Fear of exposing operator inconsistency
3. Concern about adoption by floor staff
4. Budget sensitivity due to thin margins
5. Hardware aversion due to past installs
6. Belief that equipment (not execution) is the constraint

---

## Hidden Blockers

1. Operator ego and fear of accountability
2. Leadership misalignment between ops and finance
3. Burnout causing resistance to change
4. Prior consultant trauma
5. Belief that variability is unavoidable
6. Fear of being proven wrong

---

## Sales Approaches That DON'T WORK

- Lead with AI language too early
- Pitch full platform before trust is built
- Over-explain features instead of outcomes
- Compare directly to dashboards
- Push ROI math before clarity is established

---

## What WORKS

1. Frame Newton as **visibility, not optimization**
2. Emphasize **"minutes not days"**
3. Show that Newton **observes, not judges**
4. Position as a **diagnostic layer**
5. Separate: **Record → Explain → Prevent** (stages of trust)
6. Sell **relief, not performance**

---

## Buying Moment (When They Convert)

They buy when:

- They admit they don't know why yields moved
- They feel pressure to explain inconsistency
- They want fewer operator debates
- They want confidence BEFORE the batch ends
- They realize post-mortems are too late

---

## High-Maturity Segment (Elite Operators)

### Who They Are

- Top-tier operators in their state
- Strong brands where consistency matters
- Already profitable but exposed to drift
- Operations mature but strained
- Leadership values precision and control

### Core Tension

These teams are NOT failing. They're afraid of slowly losing ground without knowing when or why.

### Their Real Fear

- Silent degradation of execution
- Variability that doesn't trigger alarms
- Operators doing different things on different shifts
- Brand damage before anyone notices
- Discovering issues after product ships

### Why Newton Resonates for Them

- Detects shifts while the batch runs
- Doesn't require process change
- Doesn't rely on operator interpretation
- Removes ambiguity without blame
- Gives leadership early warning

### How They Respond

They convert FASTER because:
- They understand the cost of drift
- They value early signals
- They already think in systems
- They respond to clarity framing
- They see Newton as **insurance, not software**

## Internal Champion Profile

- Former engineer or systems thinker
- Ops leader tired of firefighting
- COO accountable for margin stability
- Director who hates post-mortems

---

## How Newton Must Be Positioned

**Newton IS:**
- Real-time operational visibility
- A feedback compression system
- A way to know before it costs you

**Newton IS NOT:**
- Optimization
- AI
- Another tool

---

## CRITICAL RULE for Sales

> If a prospect is comfortable discovering problems after the batch finishes, they are not ready.
> If a prospect is uncomfortable not knowing what is happening during execution, Newton fits.

> If a prospect is comfortable with variability, they are not the ICP.
> If a prospect feels tension around not knowing, they are.

---

## Non-ICP Conditions

True Non-ICP (they will NOT buy):

- Teams not experiencing tension around variability
- Operators and leadership comfortable waiting for yield results
- Organizations that don't feel pain from delayed feedback
- Teams that accept not knowing during the run
- Leadership that doesn't feel exposed by uncertainty
- Buyers seeking validation rather than clarity
- Organizations that believe post-mortems are sufficient

---

## IMPORTANT CLARIFICATION

**Single-system operators CAN be ICP** — size is not the constraint
**Early SOP teams CAN be ICP** — maturity is not the constraint
**Plant variability believers CAN be ICP** — mindset changes with pain

**The constraint is TENSION:**
They become ICP when uncertainty creates friction, not when they hit arbitrary size/maturity thresholds.

## Wedges (Different Entry Angles)

### Operational Wedge
Teams cannot clearly explain variability.

### Economic Wedge
Hidden inefficiencies reduce yield and throughput.

### Compliance Wedge
Future regulatory environments will require better process records.

### Leadership Wedge
Executives want consistency across operators and facilities.

**Strongest wedge should shape the outreach.**
