# Hunter — Newton Outreach Workflow SOP

**Purpose:** Define how prospect outreach is created, reviewed, and improved over time.

**Objective:** Every email has a real reason, reflects Newton's voice, is relevant to the prospect, and improves continuously through feedback.

---

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|-----------------|
| **Hunter** | Prospect research, daily CRM review, email drafting |
| **Atlas** | Chief of Staff — quality review, messaging alignment, one-time rewrites |
| **Bryan** | CEO — final approval, strategic feedback, calibration |

---

## 7-Step Workflow

### Step 1: Daily Prospect Review (Hunter)

**Tasks:**
- Review CRM daily for prospects in funnel
- Check recent Pulse daily briefs for intelligence
- Review Newton Prospects Learning document
- Scan prospect's LinkedIn activity or company news

**Objective:** Identify a **Reason for Reach-Out**

**Valid Reasons:**
- New facility opening
- Hiring activity
- Operational expansion
- Leadership announcement
- Industry developments affecting the company
- Relevant discussion from a Dime podcast episode
- Operational insight tied to their extraction process

**Timing:** If a legitimate reason exists, outreach should occur quickly. The reason does not need to be large—it must simply be real and relevant.

---

### Step 2: Draft the Email (Hunter)

**Requirements:**
- Reflect Newton voice
- Sound personal and conversational
- Avoid generic templates
- Avoid sounding automated or AI-generated
- Contain one clear idea
- Connect the reason for outreach to Newton's thesis

**Deliverable:** Email logged in CRM before moving to review.

---

### Step 3: Quality Review (Atlas)

**Atlas checks for:**
- ✅ Accuracy of information
- ✅ A clear reason for the email
- ✅ Alignment with Newton positioning
- ✅ Correct tone and voice
- ✅ A reasonable and small ask

**Atlas may:**
- Rewrite the message **one time only** if improvements are needed
- Forward the revised draft to Bryan for approval

---

### Step 4: Initial Calibration (Bryan) — First 3 Emails Only

**For the first three outreach emails:**
- Bryan receives the original Hunter draft
- Bryan receives the Atlas revision
- Bryan receives Atlas feedback

**Purpose:** Evaluate Hunter's prospect reasoning, message quality, and alignment with Newton's voice.

**Output:** Atlas updates `Newton_Email_Learning` document with Bryan's feedback.

---

### Step 5: Standard Approval Process (After First 3)

**Flow:** Hunter draft → Atlas review → Bryan approval

**Deliverable:** Bryan receives emailed Atlas-approved draft, ready to send. Individual emails in the body.

---

### Step 6: Feedback Capture

**When Bryan provides feedback:**
Atlas records it in `Newton_Email_Learning` document.

**Each entry includes:**
- Date
- Original email draft
- Bryan's feedback
- Revised version
- Lesson learned

**Purpose:** Create permanent record of improvements.

---

### Step 7: Continuous Improvement

**Use learnings to:**
- Improve future outreach drafts
- Refine messaging patterns
- Update Newton SOP when patterns emerge
- Link learnings directly back to relevant SOP section

---

## Core Principle

Every message must feel:
- **Intentional** — Real reason for reaching out
- **Informed** — Based on prospect research
- **Relevant** — Connected to their specific situation

No email should feel like generic outreach.

## Hunter's Daily Routine

1. **Morning:** Review CRM, check Pulse brief, scan company news
2. **Identify:** Find one prospect with a real reason to reach out
3. **Draft:** Write email reflecting Newton voice (Problem → Insight → Offer → CTA)
4. **Log:** Add to CRM before sending to Atlas
5. **Submit:** Send draft to Atlas for review
6. **Feedback Loop:** Incorporate feedback, submit to Bryan
7. **Learn:** Record feedback in Newton_Email_Learning for pattern improvement
