# HVLP Gym/Club Operator — #1 Challenge & Educational Opportunity

**Category:** [General] — Industry Research  
**Date:** 2026-02-14  

---

## #1 Challenge: Member Retention Beyond 90 Days

High-volume, low-price (HVLP) operators (Planet Fitness, Crunch, EōS, Blink, Chuze, etc.) have cracked acquisition — $10–25/mo removes the price barrier and drives massive sign-ups. But the model's Achilles heel is **keeping members past the first 90 days without relying on "ghost members" who pay but never show.**

### Why it's the #1 problem:
- **Attrition runs 30–50% annually** across the HVLP segment. Churn is baked into the model.
- **Ghost revenue is unreliable** — regulators and payment processors are tightening cancellation rules (FTC "click to cancel," state-level billing transparency laws like CA and NY).
- **Engaged members spend more** — upgrades, personal training, retail, supplements. An engaged $25/mo member is worth 3–5x a ghost $10/mo member over their lifetime.
- **Competition is intensifying** — more HVLP brands entering every MSA. Differentiation on price alone is a race to zero.

### The retention gap specifically:
- Most HVLP clubs have **no structured onboarding** past a quick tour
- Staff are trained on sales and front-desk ops, **not behavior change or member engagement**
- Programming is generic — no segmentation by experience level, goals, or barriers
- Technology (apps, check-in data) is underutilized for early-warning churn detection

---

## What an Educational Institution Can Do — Specific Plays

### 1. Certified Retention Specialist Program
- Train club managers and key staff on **behavioral psychology of habit formation** (first 66 days)
- Teach data-driven member engagement: how to use check-in frequency, app usage, and class attendance as leading indicators
- Certification adds credibility and career development for staff (reduces *staff* turnover too)

### 2. Onboarding System Design Course
- Teach clubs how to build a **structured 30-60-90 day new member journey**
- Include: welcome sequences, goal-setting touchpoints, milestone recognition, re-engagement triggers
- Provide templates and playbooks they can deploy immediately

### 3. "HVLP Programming That Retains" Workshop
- How to design group programming that scales at high volume but still creates **community and accountability**
- Small group training models, challenge programs, beginner-specific tracks
- Revenue impact modeling: show operators the dollar value of moving retention 5%

### 4. Leadership & Culture Training for GMs
- HVLP clubs often promote from within with minimal leadership development
- Teach: team engagement, member-facing culture, how to shift from "sell memberships" to "build members"
- Directly tied to reducing both member and employee churn

### 5. Data & Technology Utilization
- Most clubs sit on goldmines of check-in and usage data they don't act on
- Teach: churn prediction basics, segmentation, automated outreach triggers
- No-code / low-code tools that a club manager can actually implement
