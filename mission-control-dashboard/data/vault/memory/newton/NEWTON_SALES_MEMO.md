## Core Sales Thesis

**Newton is not sold as software. Newton is sold as CLARITY.**

Most extraction teams don't struggle because they're careless. They struggle because **they don't know why results change until it's too late**.

### The Problem

- **Yield is a lagging indicator**
- Teams discover performance changes **DAYS AFTER a run**
- Multiple additional batches may be processed under the same assumptions
- By the time root cause is identified, margin is already gone

### Newton's Solution

**Shorten the feedback loop.**

Newton helps teams understand why performance moves **earlier**, allowing them to:
- Stabilize output across operators, shifts, and batches
- Move from post-mortems to prevention
- Reduce cognitive load on senior staff

### Core Framing That Resonates

- Identical inputs, different yields
- Yield is too late
- Find out why performance moves in **minutes, not days**
- Operators are already doing most of the work
- Newton simply **organizes and surfaces the signal**

## Sales Approach & Tactics

### 1. Lead With the Problem

**The most effective conversations begin with variability and delayed feedback.**

Instead of presenting technology, conversations start by asking:
- How do you currently discover when a run underperforms?
- Why do results change?
- How long does it take to understand the issue?

Once the gap is visible, Newton becomes the natural solution.

### 2. Diagnostic Selling

**Objective of early calls is NOT to pitch quickly.**

**Objective is to understand:**
- What variables change most often
- How operators currently track them
- How long it takes to detect problems
- How confident the team is in their current process

**Goal:** Get the prospect to explain the operational gap themselves.

### 3. Software First, Hardware Later

**CRITICAL lesson learned.**

Best sequence:
1. Software and operational logging
2. Run comparisons and visibility
3. Pattern recognition
4. Real-time monitoring and hardware

This lowers adoption friction and avoids early conversations about installation risk.

### 4. Record, Explain, Prevent (Tier Progression)

This structure mirrors how trust builds within facilities:

**Tier 1 — Record**
- Operators log runs and parameters

**Tier 2 — Explain**
- System reveals patterns, comparisons, and variability

**Tier 3 — Prevent**
- Real-time monitoring and alerts reduce mistakes mid-run

### 5. Health Check Positioning

Rather than pitching a large transformation, frame Newton as:
- A short operational health check
- A visibility layer for the process
- A way to identify patterns across runs and operators

This lowers resistance and makes first engagement easier.

### 6. Wedge Strategy (Different Entry Angles)

Match the strongest wedge to the prospect:

**Operational wedge**
- Teams cannot clearly explain variability

**Economic wedge**
- Hidden inefficiencies reduce yield and throughput

**Compliance wedge**
- Future regulatory environments will require better process records

**Leadership wedge**
- Executives want consistency across operators and facilities

## Common Sales Objections & Responses

### "Variability Is Normal"

Many operators believe yield changes are unavoidable.

**Response approach:**
- Do NOT argue
- Focus on SPEED of understanding
- Reframe: The issue isn't variability itself. The issue is how quickly the team can explain it.

### "Not Enough Data"

Many facilities have inconsistent records.

**Response approach:**
- Meet them where they are
- Start with simple logging and structured visibility

### "No Time"

Operators are frequently overloaded.

**Response approach:**
- Position Newton as REDUCING analysis work, not adding new tasks

### "Burned by Previous Tools"

Many operators distrust software and consultants.

**Response approach:**
- Avoid hype
- Stay grounded in operational language
- Avoid AI terminology early

### "Budget Constraints"

Cash pressure is common in the industry.

**Response approach:**
- Encourage phased adoption
- Offer small initial commitments

### "Fear of Disruption"

Operators worry about interfering with active runs.

**Response approach:**
- Emphasize MINIMAL workflow changes
- Highlight passive operator experience

### "Spreadsheet Comfort"

Some teams believe spreadsheets are sufficient.

**Response approach:**

Focus on what spreadsheets CANNOT do easily:
- Automatic comparisons
- Drift detection
- Structured records across teams

## Key Market Lessons

### Trust Develops in Stages

Do NOT introduce the full product vision immediately.

1. Demonstrate understanding of the operation
2. Solve one clear gap
3. Then expand to adjacent problems

### Explanation Comes Before Automation

Most teams first need help UNDERSTANDING their process before they can automate it.

### Simplicity Wins

**Strong promise:** Newton helps teams understand why performance moves.

Anything more complex creates friction.

### Speak Operationally

Extraction teams respond to operational clarity, not academic language.

Focus on:
- Fewer surprises
- Stable output
- Faster learning loops

### Internal Politics Matter

**Some sales stalls are NOT technical.**

Common hidden blockers:
- Leadership disagreement
- Operator resistance
- Budget control conflicts

Understanding the decision environment is CRITICAL.

## Prospect Nurturing Principles

### Conversations Need Context

Cold follow-ups rarely work without a reason.

Effective nurture assets include:
- Relevant podcast discussions
- Industry insights
- Operational observations

**Each message should connect directly to the prospect's context.**

### One Idea Per Message

Each email should deliver ONE clear concept.

Avoid combining multiple objectives in one message.

### Prospect Memory

For EACH company track:
- Extraction type
- Facility maturity
- Equipment and process
- Decision makers
- Constraints and objections
- Strongest sales wedge

This improves future follow-up quality.

## Sales Tactics Summary

### What WORKS

1. **Lead with the problem** — Variability, delayed feedback, cost of not knowing
2. **Diagnostic selling** — Ask questions; let them identify the gap
3. **Software first** — Avoid hardware conversations early
4. **Record → Explain → Prevent** — Build trust through progression
5. **Health check framing** — Low-stakes entry point
6. **Match the wedge** — Operational, economic, compliance, or leadership angle
7. **Stay operational** — No AI, no hype, no optimization talk
8. **Emphasize minutes, not days** — Speed of feedback is the key differentiator
9. **One idea per email** — Clarity and simplicity
10. **Personalize by role** — Not by personality or flattery

### What DOESN'T WORK

1. Lead with AI language too early
2. Pitch full platform before trust is built
3. Over-explain features instead of outcomes
4. Compare directly to dashboards
5. Push ROI math before clarity is established
6. Use forbidden phrases (reaching out, leveraging, etc.)
7. Generic outreach without a specific reason
8. Enthusiasm or marketing tone
9. Multiple ideas in one email
10. Scheduling links or demo offers
