## 👥 AUDIENCE PERSONAS

### Persona 1: "The Operator" (Primary — ~45% of audience)
**Who they are:** Multi-state or single-state operators, COOs, VP Ops, production managers. In the weeds daily. Been in the industry 3-7 years.
**What they want:** Tactical insight. How other operators solved the same problems they have. What works at scale. What breaks.
**Content they engage with:** Operations, extraction, manufacturing, consistency, scaling pre-roll/production
**Evidence:**
- "The Hidden Weakness in Most Cannabis Operations" → 1,342 views (#1 long-form)
- "Fix the Process: Murphy Murri on Manufacturing Optimization" → 1,361 views
- "How to Scale Pre-Roll Production" → 46v but 15.2% like ratio (small audience, very qualified)
- Science/tech shorts avg 200 views vs. 56 for business ops — they want HOW, not WHAT
**Title triggers:** weakness, hidden, broken, scale, process, consistency, efficiency
**Title killers:** "holistic approach", "strategic overview", anything that sounds like a conference panel

---

### Persona 2: "The Investor/Analyst" (Secondary — ~25% of audience)
**Who they are:** Cannabis-focused fund managers, analysts, MFOs with cannabis exposure. Also retail investors tracking the space.
**What they want:** Market signals, financial health of operators, macro trends, debt/capital dynamics.
**Content they engage with:** Finance, market structure, policy that affects returns, company-specific analysis
**Evidence:**
- "Is SNDL a Silent Giant?" → 593v, 5.6% like ratio (highest engagement by ratio in long-form)
- "Tier 1s, Cannabis Debt Crisis, and the 2026 Reset" → 438v, 5.7% ratio
- "No Exits, No Capital, No Bullsh*t: Seth Yakatan" → 1,207 views
- "Storytelling Is the Future of Cannabis IR" → 1,255 views
**Title triggers:** debt, capital, reset, crisis, exits, hidden, where's the cash, silent giant
**Title killers:** vague market optimism, anything that doesn't have stakes

---

### Persona 3: "The Policy Watcher" (Tertiary — ~15% of audience)
**Who they are:** Lobbyists, compliance officers, multistate attorneys, operators watching legislative windows.
**What they want:** Concrete policy implications — not analysis for its own sake, but what it means for their business.
**Content they engage with:** Rescheduling, 280E, state-by-state moves, enforcement signals
**Evidence:**
- "Ohio's Adult Use Cannabis Struggles" → 237v
- "Rescheduling favors built infrastructure" → 131v (compressed, consequence-first)
- "Pharma failed where relief mattered most" → 1,117v (policy + human stakes = viral)
- "Enforcement decides growth" → 22% like ratio, 18 views (super-qualified, low reach)
**Title triggers:** what enforcement means, who wins if X passes, Ohio, rescheduling, 280E ending
**Title killers:** abstract policy analysis, academic framing

---

### Persona 4: "The Brand Builder" (Emerging — ~10% of audience)
**Who they are:** CMOs, brand directors, dispensary GMs building consumer-facing identity. Newer to the industry or crossing over from CPG.
**What they want:** How top brands think, what makes products win in retail, consumer education strategies.
**Content they engage with:** Brand positioning, retail strategy, consumer behavior, label/packaging
**Evidence:**
- "Why the Industry's Premier Brands Choose The Flowery ft. Ilya" → 308v, 5.2% ratio
- "Simplify Cannabis Labels for Mass Education" → 52v, 3.8% ratio
- "Cannabis: Learning from Food Industry" → 32v, 3.1% ratio
**Title triggers:** how [top brand] does it, what premium brands know, why consumers choose
**Title killers:** generic "branding tips" framing

| 357 | Tobacco Companies: Exploring Alternatives | Tobacco |

**Why:** Audience is cannabis-native but curious about how other industries see them. It's also a discovery mechanism — people searching tobacco/pharma/AI topics land on cannabis content.
**Action:** Every guest interview should include 1 question about their experience in/with adjacent industries.

## 📐 COPY & POSITIONING RULES FOR NEW EPISODES

### Short-form Titles
1. **Lead with consequence** — what breaks, what's hidden, what's being missed
2. **3-6 words max** — compressed outperforms paragraph titles
3. **No colons** — "Guest Name: Topic Here" kills curiosity
4. **Lowercase or sentence case** — matches native short-form feel
5. **Use outside-in when possible** — Tobacco, Pharma, Alcohol, AI as lens

### Long-form Titles
1. **Declarative > Question > Colon** — statements with stakes outperform questions
2. **ft. Guest name works** — only when the name has recognition in the space
3. **Pipe format for authority** — "Topic | Guest Name" signals credibility
4. **Never bury the insight** — the thesis goes first, not the guest name

### Content Planning
- Book more guests from: extraction/tech, finance/capital markets, adjacent industries (tobacco, pharma, beverage)
- Dedicate at least 1 short per episode to the "hidden weakness" or "consequence" angle
- Track which clips from each episode perform — the highest-performing short = the topic/angle to build next episode around
