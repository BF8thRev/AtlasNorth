# Newton Sales Playbook
*Last updated: 2026-02-14*

## What Newton Actually Sells
Newton sells **closed-loop operational review with built-in accountability**.
- Not data logging (everyone has that — SharePoint, spreadsheets)
- Not "catching one bad batch"
- It sells: timing, visibility, institutional memory, and a decision path

**Core differentiator vs. SharePoint/legacy tools:**
Legacy tools are *archaeological* — they show what happened after the fact with no answers.
Newton is *operational* — it shows what to do next and tracks whether anyone acted.

## Prospect Personas

### 1. COO / VP of Operations (PRIMARY TARGET — THE BUYER)
- **Cares about:** Margin, efficiency, risk reduction, accountability across facilities
- **Pain they feel:** Can't see operational drift until it's extreme. Doesn't know if teams are following process or winging it.
- **How to reach:** LinkedIn + personalized Loom video. Research frame ("I'm studying how multi-facility operators handle post-processing review loops").
- **Language that works:** "Your teams are logging data nobody reviews." / "One 3% yield drop across 50 runs/year at your throughput = $X."
- **What makes them act:** Compounding cost math, accountability/insurance framing, peer comparison ("here's what the best operators do differently")
- **Entry ask:** 15-minute research conversation, NOT a demo

### 2. Director of Processing / Decision Maker (SECONDARY — THE INTERNAL CHAMPION)
- **Cares about:** Making their team's output better, having clear answers on what to change
- **Pain they feel:** Too many variables, no decision framework, post-processing data arrives late, loop never closes
- **How to reach:** Warm intro from COO (ask: "Who owns the extraction-to-post-processing handoff?")
- **Language that works:** "Newton gives your team a clear decision path instead of guessing across 20 variables"
- **Role in deal:** Validates the need internally, becomes the daily user advocate

### 3. Extraction Technician (DO NOT TARGET DIRECTLY FOR SALES)
- **Reality:** Not by a computer. Not a buyer. Not checking email consistently.
- **Role in deal:** End user, not decision maker. Reaches them AFTER COO + Director buy-in.
- **Key insight from pilots:** Even when techs log data, they're not the reviewer — so the loop never closes. The problem isn't the tech, it's the system.

### Jared | DMC — Diagnostic Miner / CEO-Level Framing

### Dom | Verano — Firefighter / CO2 Workflow Need
- **Date:** Pre-Feb 2026
- **Target:** Dom at Verano — military-style operator, always putting out fires, strong technical confidence
- **Method:** Respect-first, system-level reframing, yield as lagging indicator, validated his firefighter identity. No pitch.
- **Result:** Opened up — admitted firefighting, shared constraints and bottlenecks deeply, showed vulnerability.
- **Objections surfaced:** Believes he's on top of metrics. Reviews dashboard multiple times/day. Yield already triggers investigation. Not open to considering anything outside his current focus.
- **Core blocker:** Believes reaction speed = control. Currently looking for a **CO2 workflow tool** — Newton isn't ready out of the box for that, and that's been the hold-up.
- **Learning:** Do NOT imply he's missing data. Reframe around *timing gap* not *efficiency*. His identity is speed — Newton must feel like it makes him *faster*, not *smarter*. But the real blocker is product-market fit: he needs CO2 workflow and we don't have it ready.
- **Status:** 🟠 Stalled on product gap — CO2 workflow not ready
- **Next move:** Two options: (1) Fast-track CO2 workflow to unblock the deal, or (2) Park Dom until it's ready and re-engage with "we built what you asked for." Don't keep pushing without the product.

### Leef — Pilot A (Large Operator)
- **Date:** Pre-Feb 2026
- **Target:** Large multi-facility operator. This was **Pilot 1.**
- **Method:** High-level strategic framing, yield visibility, system monitoring
- **Result:** Operators logged data, but **no manager ever reviewed it**. Nobody closed the loop. No one made decisions based on the data.
- **Core failure mode:** Classic Pilot A pattern — logging without review. The tool worked. Adoption at the operator level happened. But the review/decision layer above never engaged.
- **Learning:** Product worked. Adoption worked at tech level. The failure was organizational — no accountability loop, no reviewer, no ownership of the output.
- **Duration:** ~1 year. Passed through multiple POCs. Nobody ever owned the data.
- **Status:** 🔴 Dead pilot — exhausted multiple entry points over a year, organizational ownership never materialized
- **Next move:** Not worth re-engaging the same way. If re-approach, must be at exec level with a fundamentally different frame — or use as a case study (anonymized) for other prospects.

### Ashley (Liz) | Marimed — Operations Leadership / Cross-Functional
- **Date:** Pre-Feb 2026
- **Target:** Operations leadership (Ashley) — not deep extraction, closer to cross-functional oversight
- **Method:** Operational clarity framing, system visibility, yield variability discussion, light ROI framing
- **Result:** Conceptual agreement that variability exists. Acknowledged teams are stretched. Interest in structure.
- **Objections surfaced:** Bandwidth concerns. Change fatigue. Fear of operator pushback. Not convinced implementation friction is low.
- **Core blocker:** She's not fighting machines — she's fighting *people, adoption, and capacity.* Worries about: who has to touch this, what breaks, who gets blamed, how much time it costs.
- **Learning:** Ashley-type buyers don't care about "better yield" or "efficiency." They care about: **reduced cognitive load, fewer escalations, less babysitting, cleaner reporting up.** Frame Newton as *operational quiet* and *managerial clarity*.
- **Status:** 🟡 Exploratory — needs friction-reduction proof, not ROI proof
- **Next move:** Show her exactly how low-touch the implementation is. Ideally: "Your techs log in 2 minutes, you get a weekly summary, zero escalations unless something actually matters."

## Pipeline Segments

### Tier 1 — Active Deals (Separate Motion)
- 3 large multi-system players, timing stage
- **Action needed:** Create urgency trigger (journalism piece, scarcity play, new case study)

### Tier 2 — Warm but Stalled (~6 prospects)
- Know about Newton, conversations started but dropped off
- **Action needed:** Reconnect with a fresh concept or angle — not "checking in" but a reason to re-engage (journalism piece, new case study, specific insight relevant to them)

- No relationship, hard to gain access
- **Action needed:** Automated nurture sequence (monthly, problem-aware content) to warm them up. NOT individual cold emails.

### Tier 4 — Inbound / Warm Referrals
- Podcast listeners, journalism readers, intro requests
- **Action needed:** Clear CTA funnels, lead magnet, fast follow-up process

## Learnings & Rules

1. Never cold email extraction technicians. They're not at computers and don't buy.
2. COO/VP Ops is the entry point. They introduce you down to the operator level.
3. Don't sell the tool — sell the closed loop. Everyone already logs data.
4. Compounding cost math > single-batch stories.
5. The research frame ("I'm studying how operators handle X") opens more doors than a pitch.
6. Every podcast guest is a potential prospect or connector. Follow up within 48 hrs.
7. When a pilot stalls, use time-spent-per-user data to show WHERE the loop broke — reframes from "tool didn't work" to "team didn't close the loop."
8. SharePoint comparison is a kill shot — use it every time.
9. **Identity-protective prospects require leverage framing.** "Expand capability" not "fix weakness." Never imply a gap — imply an unlock. (Source: Jared/DMC)
10. **Firefighter-identity operators need speed framing.** Newton must feel like it makes them *faster*, not *smarter*. Reframe timing gap, never imply they're missing data. (Source: Dom/Statehouse)
11. **Two buyer archetypes emerging:** (a) The Analytical CEO who needs to feel like they're *choosing* to level up, and (b) The Hands-On Operator who needs to feel like Newton amplifies their speed, not replaces their judgment.
12. Diagnostic/research-style conversations generate more depth than any pitch. Both Jared and Dom opened up significantly under this frame.
13. **Third buyer archetype: The Overwhelmed Manager.** (Source: Ashley/Marimed) Doesn't care about yield numbers or tech. Cares about: fewer escalations, less babysitting, cleaner reporting up. Frame Newton as *operational quiet*.
14. **Leef was a product success / organizational failure.** Operators adopted. Management didn't review. The tool isn't the problem — the accountability layer is. Re-engage at management level with their own usage data.
14b. **When a prospect needs a specific workflow you don't have (CO2 for Dom/Verano), stop pushing.** Either build it or park the deal. Pushing without the product erodes the relationship.
15. **"Don't want another dashboard" is the most common proxy objection.** It really means "don't add to my cognitive load." Answer with the closed-loop frame, not feature lists.
