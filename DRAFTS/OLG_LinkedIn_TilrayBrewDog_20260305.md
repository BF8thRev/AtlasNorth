## Draft

When Tilray bought BrewDog, nobody called it a cannabis play. They called it a "platform for adjacencies."

That's what happens when your growth story breaks.

For years, every major MSO CEO said the same thing: Big Alcohol will buy cannabis. Tobacco will buy cannabis. Legacy consumer brands will *have* to diversify into THC because their core business is shrinking.

It was always backwards.

What actually happened? The flow inverted. Cannabis companies, desperate to prove they're something *other* than commodity growers, started buying legacy brands. Tilray didn't buy BrewDo because they found a distribution lock-in. They bought it because they needed a narrative—proof that they could build legacy brands, not just extract and sell.

It's grief disguised as strategy.

The operators watching this and pivoting hardest into "adjacencies" (beverages, wellness, CPG) aren't expanding. They're demonstrating sophistication in public while their core margins collapse in private.

That positioning matters. Not because it's a lie—because it's a *performance*, and performances reveal who's still performing and who's actually managing.

What are you measuring right now? Margin? Market cap? Or the narrative?
