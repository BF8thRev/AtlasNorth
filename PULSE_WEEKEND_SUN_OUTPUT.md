## 📖 CONCEPT TO LEARN

**The OODA Loop** — a decision framework developed by U.S. Air Force Colonel John Boyd after studying why certain fighter pilots won dogfights against objectively superior opponents. OODA stands for Observe, Orient, Decide, Act. The insight isn't the four steps — it's that the pilot who completes the loop *faster* controls the engagement, because by the time the opponent reacts to your last move, you've already made three more. Boyd discovered that speed through the "Orient" phase (how you interpret what you see, filtered through your mental models, culture, and past experience) is the real differentiator — it's where most people slow down. In business, the company that can observe a market shift, reframe their understanding of it, make a call, and execute before competitors have finished debating what they observed — that company sets the tempo of the entire market. Whoever controls the tempo controls the outcome.

**Here's the metaphor:** Two chess players of equal skill sit down. One thinks for 10 minutes per move. The other thinks for 30 seconds. By the time the slow player executes their optimal strategy, the fast player has adapted three times, and the board no longer matches the slow player's plan. The fast player didn't have to be smarter — they just made the slow player's intelligence irrelevant.

## 👦 SON CHALLENGE — Sunday Edition

**The Sock Bowling Alley**

**What you need:** 6–8 empty water bottles (or toilet paper rolls standing upright), 3–4 pairs of socks rolled into balls, a hallway or open floor space.

**How to play:** Line up the bottles in a triangle like a bowling lane at one end of the hallway. Mark a "throwing line" with a piece of tape about 6–8 feet back. Take turns rolling or tossing the sock balls underhand to knock down as many bottles as possible. Reset after each turn. Add a scoring rule: 3 points for a clean strike, 1 point per bottle knocked. First to 10 wins.

**What it builds:** Underhand throwing mechanics, aim calibration, spatial distance judgment, and (bonus) early number recognition if you do the scoring together out loud. It's also a perfect Sunday wind-down — calm energy, shared laughs, no setup required beyond what's already in your house.
