## THE CONTENT STRESS TEST
*Every new piece must pass at least 2 of these 4 tests before publishing.*

### ✅ Test 1: CROSSOVER TEST
Does this topic matter to someone who doesn't follow cannabis?
- AI, Tobacco, Alcohol, Healthcare, Investing, Europe, Federal policy = PASS
- Cannabis-only with no bridge to the outside world = FLAG
- **Why:** The channel's ceiling is the cannabis bubble unless content travels. The data proves it every time.

### ✅ Test 2: CONSEQUENCE TEST
Does the title state what happens if you ignore this?
- "Acquisition hides the loss" = PASS (loss is the consequence)
- "When 280E Ends, Retail Margins Reset" = PASS (margin impact stated)
- "Hemp Distribution Expands Beverage Reach" = FAIL (no stakes)
- **Formula:** [Subject] + [what happens to them]

### ✅ Test 3: SPECIFICITY TEST
Is there a number, name, or mechanism in the title?
- "How 1% Add Millions" = PASS
- "Caleb Counts' $3.5M R&D Program" = PASS
- "7,000 NJ Liquor Stores Can Now Sell THC" = PASS
- "Solving Global Business Challenges" = FAIL
- **Why:** Specificity is credibility. Vague = skip.

### ✅ Test 4: TENSION TEST
Is there a contrast, conflict, or surprise?
- "Hemp vs Cannabis: Consumer Perspective" = PASS (vs framing)
- "Tobacco's Late Innovation" = PASS (unexpected actor + late = too slow)
- "No Exits, No Capital, No Bullsh*t" = PASS (raw honesty)
- "Cannabis: Medical to Recreational Shift" = FAIL (no tension)
- **Why:** Tension is the reason to click. No tension = no click.

### 6. Title Patterns That Win vs. Lose

- `[Company] [what they do]` — press release, not insight
- Passive constructions ("is shifting", "are exploring", "navigating")

## WHAT'S ACTUALLY MISSING

The content quality is real. The gap is **distribution mechanics**:

1. **No series structure** — Every episode is standalone. No reason to binge. No "if you liked this, the full episode covers X." The channel has no compound growth mechanism.

2. **Shorts are too long** — 83 of 161 shorts are 51-70s. That's the worst zone. A 15-second cut of the best line almost always outperforms a 60-second summary.

3. **No title consistency** — Sometimes the title is a thesis ("Acquisition hides the loss"), sometimes it's a category label ("Cannabinoid Formulations: Targeted Effects"). The algorithm can't learn who to show your content to.

4. **Crossover angles are being left on the table** — Every guest who touches Tobacco, Finance, AI, Healthcare, Europe, or Federal policy should have their crossover angle in the short title. It's happening inconsistently.

5. **Like ratio content isn't being repurposed** — The 14%-like episodes are sitting there untouched. Those are the ones worth clipping for LinkedIn, X, email.
