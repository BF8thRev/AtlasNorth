# Newton Prospects Learning — Prospecting SOP

**Source:** Newton Prospects Learning Google Doc
**Updated:** 2026-03-06
**Category:** [Newton] — Sales & Prospecting

---

## Purpose
This SOP defines how prospect outreach should be prepared and executed for Newton Insights. Every outreach message must be informed, relevant, and consistent with Newton's positioning.

---

## 1. Source of Truth for Prospect Intelligence

**Document:** Newton Prospects Learning (live operational record)

Must contain:
- **Dated entries** — Every entry must have a date
- **Source attribution** — Where did the insight come from?
- **Clear language** — Describe the insight or change explicitly

Tracks:
- Insights from Pulse daily briefs
- Industry developments
- Prospect-specific observations
- Bryan's feedback from calls
- Updates to Newton messaging/positioning

This is institutional memory for prospect learning.

---

## 2. Understanding Newton Before Outreach

**Resources folder:** Newton Background Folder (G-Drive)

Review before ANY outreach:
- Newton thesis
- Operational problem Newton solves
- Messaging principles
- Positioning strategy

**Golden Rule:** All outreach must remain aligned with these principles.

---

## 3. CRM Research Requirements

Before drafting ANY prospect email, research includes:

**Company Context:**
- Company overview
- Extraction method (if known)
- Facility size/maturity
- Recent organizational news
- Leadership structure
- Previous conversations/outreach attempts

**Prospect Persona:**
- Extraction operator
- Lab manager
- Operations director
- Executive leadership

**Rule:** Messaging must reflect the priorities of the recipient's role.

---

## 4. Outreach Preparation

Each message must have a CLEAR REASON. Acceptable reasons:
- Operational insight relevant to the company
- Industry development connected to their work
- Something discussed on a podcast episode
- Pattern identified through Newton research
- Relevant operational challenge common in their extraction type

**NEVER generic outreach.**

---

## 5. Drafting Prospect Emails

Email must:
- Reflect Newton writing style (see NEWTON_STYLE_GUIDE.md)
- Be direct and concise
- Contain ONE clear idea
- Reference a relevant reason for reaching out
- Make a small, reasonable request

Examples of acceptable requests:
- A short conversation
- Feedback on an idea
- An introduction to another operator

Emails should feel **personal and intentional**, not mass outreach.

---

## 6. Information Integrity — MANDATORY

**NEVER:**
- Fabricate data
- Assume facts about a prospect
- Exaggerate Newton capabilities
- Imply results that cannot be supported

**Rule:** If information cannot be verified, it must not be used.

Newton credibility depends on disciplined communication.

---

## 7. Awareness of Industry Context

Outreach should demonstrate awareness of relevant industry developments:
- Regulatory changes
- Operational challenges within extraction
- Shifts in market conditions
- New company initiatives/announcements

**Must be factual and verifiable** — no assumptions.

---

## 8. Alignment With Newton Messaging

Newton is framed as:
- An operational visibility platform
- A system helping teams understand process variability
- A tool stabilizing output across runs and operators

**NEVER position Newton as:**
- A miracle solution
- A guaranteed optimization engine
- An AI product with unrealistic claims

Stay grounded and credible.

---

## 9. Final Review Before Sending

Confirm ALL of these before sending:
- ✅ Prospect context is accurate
- ✅ Message has a clear reason
- ✅ Tone matches Newton writing style
- ✅ Request is small and appropriate
- ✅ No claims exceed verified capability

If ANY condition is not met, revise.

---

## Quick Reference: What Works

**Do:**
- Lead with the problem (margin pressure, delayed feedback, variability)
- Frame Newton as visibility, not optimization
- Emphasize "minutes not days"
- Show Newton observes (doesn't judge)
- Position as diagnostic layer
- Separate: Record → Explain → Prevent progression
- Sell relief, not performance

**Don't:**
- Lead with AI language too early
- Pitch full platform before trust is built
- Over-explain features
- Compare directly to dashboards
- Push ROI math before clarity is established
- Use forbidden phrases (reaching out, leveraging, unlock, etc.)
